Hey dude, I got a few suggestions to make that I think would make this build overall more effective.

- Forget about Concentration, with 15 ranks you won't be able to hold one single spell or resist any Taunts, so don't bother. Get more Heal instead. I was thinking to advise you to get Taunt (and dropping both Heal and Concentration), but the way you leveling goes (and there's not much to in terms of that), you'd only use it for the first 7 levels, and then it'd only be updated at level 21 (24 if you follow all my changes). Still an option if you want to consider it.

- The 2 first attribute stats go to Charisma, but IMO they should go to Strength. Early on is when a higher Strength score will make the most difference, in terms of both AB and damage, before the BAB progression starts to become relevant. At least the first 4 Stat increases should go to Strength, then you can start taking Charisma.

- You take Divine Shield in the mid-epics as a general feat, why don't you alter your pre-epic spread to Paladin (8) / Champion of Torm (8) / Weapon Master (4), take Knockdown with that CoT bonus Feat and Divine Shield at level 18.

- Once in the epics, nothing is better in terms of AB than Epic Weapon Focus, so if you go for the new spread, you should take Epic Weapon Focus at level 21, then Armor Skin on that last Champion of Torm bonus feat, while leaving Epic Prowess to be taken at level 40.

- Use a Battleaxe, it's a Slashing weapon, so any party member can cast Keen Edge on it. If you're a solo man, or if keen weapons are common, don't bother.

I kinda disagree with this, at least partially. I am not in favor of giving advices that involve class changes, because dropping a class radically changes the build, unless it's an extreme case. Being a novice builder, you gotta explore the classes you know, and build within your means. I remember when I was starting out, many of my builds involved Weapon Master, because that extra threat range and multiplier looked too juicy to me. It's easier to know that you'll score more criticals and do more damage than it is to understand how to generate Sneak Attacks, how Death Attack works, how shifting works for Druids, know the spellbook for all the classes: you gotta start somewhere. This build is what we here in Brazil would call "feijoada" (which is basically rice and black beans). Feijoada is the most common and worldwide known Brazilian typical food. It's the basic, the simple, the ordinary. That's what this build is.

Sure the build becomes pretty much a one-trick pony and has some deficiencies because of the class-combo, but it does make sense. Paladin is the RP base of the build, CoT comes to further enhance the saves and provide the bonus feats to help match the requirements for WM. Increased multiplier and ki critical given by WM have a good synergy with Paladin damage buffs, which all (except for smites) multiply on critical hits. To me, this combo has enough synergies to make a good build .

Take it EZ! Is it a "poor" build? No. Not at all. The thing when you mix pal, cot, and wm together is, where's the diversity? While it is perfectly acceptable to do this sort of combo, the reason to combine one class with another is to get something that the other class DOESNT get.

Here, Pal gives access to Div might and spells (saves). COT gives saves and feats (ignore the other stuff cuz it isnt that important). WM gives crit damage and crit range. No tumble. No umd. etc

WHen you mix a build like this it is extremely easy to RP as each chosen class is so similar to the others that there is really no challenge in it.

I hope none of what i say is taken negatively. I dont mean it that way. I was just making the point to maybe help you for the next build by getting you to ask, "what is this build missing and how can i get some of it?" There are several points one can start from when critiquing a build. I do like to start with the conception, as, often, it is the basic concept of the build that I may find flawed (such as this one, as I don't think it's a very good combo). From there, one follows by critiquing the build with the assumption that one is trying to make the best use of the basic concept. That's the nuts and bolts. Some guys are more into the nuts and bolts, some guys like the whole pie. I'm a pie kinda guy. Point is, there's value to both those perspectives, though often, the greater picture critique is rather brief.

Anyhoo... I guess a discussion of the philosophy behind build critiques is a might bit off topic.
