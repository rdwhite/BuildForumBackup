I think going Wizard-heavy would COMPLETELY AND UTTERLY ruin the build, simply because the 2 things that I was going for (good DA DC and decent AB) would not be achieved, and the whole purpose of even taking Assassin levels would be forfeit. Going Assassin-heavy, while not granting me the initial idea of bonus from this synergy, would still grant me the following:

- Important buffs such as: True Strike, Fox's Cunning, Cat's Grace, Invisibility and Improved Invisibility (from another source), Damage Shields, Flame Weapon, Greater Magic Weapon;

- 2 Great Intelligence feats as Wizard bonus feats;

- DA DC of 35 unbuffed, 41 with capped INT (easier to achieve with Fox's Cunning).

This build as it is has a good DA DC, 26 pre-epic BAB and 42 AB, which is no small feat for a 3/4 BAB/level mixed up with a 1/2 BAB/level character.

I don't like the idea of a near-pure mage, TBH, if I were looking to build a pure mage, I would for starters go Sorcerer instead of Wizard and I'd have MANY more interesting classes to combine it with than Assassin.

As for going SD, no, I didn't really think much about that, because HiPS isn't that useful to this build (you have many forms of Invisibility and the whole idea is to have a good chance of paralyzing the opponent when you first strike from the shadows and be able to hold your own in direct combat with KD / IKD even if you don't). This build is very similar to The Hatchet by Cinnabar Din and the GrimBlade by grizzled_dwarflord, except I take Ranger instead of Fighter because I think Ranger has better synergy in that it has Hide and Move Silently as class skills, more skill points, has Fighter-type AB, Cat's Grace, and has the Camouflage spell, while Fighter only gives unnecessary bonus feats and WS / EWS.

IMO the build does get enough from each class to be quite powerful, although Evasion and Improved Evasion would be nice to have .
